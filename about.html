From shipyards to strategy

I started with ships. At the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), I learned to design vessels from the keel up — hydrostatics, stability, propulsion, the whole discipline of making steel float and move efficiently. My thesis took that further: a numerical study of semi-submersible platforms in the Bay of Bengal, now accepted for publication in the Journal of Maritime Research.

But somewhere between the lines plans and the load calculations, I discovered I was equally drawn to the business of building things — why a product succeeds, how a supply chain holds together, what makes a team deliver. That curiosity took me to a Post Graduate Diploma in Supply Chain Management (ISCEA) and an MBA in Marketing at the University of Dhaka.

Today, at Tech Marine Solutions Ltd., both halves of my training work together. I've helped launch the Vikings Boat brand — designing the factory layout as an engineer, running the market research and cost analysis as a marketer, and leading the IT team that built the company's digital presence.

Outside work, I'm a trained French speaker (DELF B1), a blood-donation volunteer with BADHON at BUET, and an occasional podcaster with opinions about how Bangladeshi society is changing.
